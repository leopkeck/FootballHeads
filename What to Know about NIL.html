Article Title

NIL, or Name, Image, and Likeness, has transpired over the past couple of years, and there are many misconceptions about the money it gives, and what is is given for. NIL money is often wrongly though of as money that is given to an athlete, just cause. The reason why this topic is alway misconcieved is because you have probably never looked into it. The simple definition, given by Icon Source, says that, "In the simplest of terms, Name, Image and Likeness (NIL) is a term that describes the means through which college athletes are allowed to receive financial compensation. NIL refers to the use of an athlete's name, image, and likeness through marketing and promotional endeavors." The real reason these deals are made is because athletes are unable to be directly paid by the NCAA or any teams that may associate with them, because they can not be given money outside of scholarships. One notable case of this problem was with the NCAA college sports games, which showed athletes likeness, until they were disbanded because they could't pay the teams or the athletes. However, EA Sports will be able to now make the games again because they can pay athletes through NIL.
